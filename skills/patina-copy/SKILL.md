# Patina Copy

Read `AGENTS.md` before writing.

Default to concise Spanish for customer-facing copy unless the surrounding surface clearly uses another language.

Write as if the brand is calm, confident, and already knows its value. Keep the copy brief enough that the object remains the focus.

## Write Like This

- Prefer short headings with clean rhythm.
- Keep support lines concise and intentional.
- Use language that feels crafted, tactile, and understated.
- Let emotion come through quietly rather than through hype.
- Keep CTA text simple, direct, and tasteful.

## Useful Length Targets

- Headings: usually 2 to 6 words.
- Supporting lines: usually 1 short sentence.
- Product blurbs: usually 1 sentence, occasionally 2 if truly needed.
- Microcopy and CTA labels: as short as clarity allows.

## Avoid

- Generic ecommerce phrases like "best seller", "shop now", "limited time", or "premium quality" used as filler.
- Over-explaining craftsmanship in every block.
- Repeating the same adjectives across the page.
- Sounding loud, trendy, or promotional.

## Product Copy Guardrails

- Preserve authenticity. Describe what is true, visible, or already grounded in the brand.
- Do not invent artistic techniques, stories, or product claims.
- Do not suggest changing the painted artwork itself unless the user explicitly asks.
- When in doubt, remove words rather than add them.

## Project Notes

- Current site copy is short, Spanish-first, and editorial.
- Match the tone already present in `app/page.tsx` and `app/_components/editorial-sections.tsx`.
- Keep labels in the catalogue and pricing surfaces clean and consistent with `app/_components/catalogue-browser.tsx` and `app/_components/pricing-summary.tsx`.

## Validation

- Read the final copy aloud once. If it sounds explained instead of composed, tighten it.
- Check nearby headings and labels so tone and cadence stay consistent.
